How To Use IronLog

Logging a set

Enter a Split (e.g. Push Day) and Exercise, then fill in up to 3 sets. As soon as you've typed a split/exercise that you've logged before, IronLog auto-fills the weight and reps from your last session with that exercise — just change whatever's different today. Pick a Date if it isn't today, then hit LOG SET.

To fix a mistake later, open that exercise's history and click the ✎ next to any session — it loads everything back into the form so you can correct and re-save it, instead of deleting and re-entering.

Normal, Drop, and Bodyweight sets

Each of the 3 set slots has its own type dropdown:

  • Normal — one weight × reps.
  • Drop set — up to 3 stages (D1 → D2 → D3), each with its own weight and reps, for a set where you drop the weight and keep going without resting. The full D1→D2→D3 breakdown always shows in your history table — but only D1 (the first, heaviest stage) counts toward average volume, the progress graphs, and PRs. D2/D3 use lighter weight to failure, so including them would water down your actual strength trend.
  • Bodyweight — for exercises like back extensions, dips, or pull-ups where there's no external load. The weight field becomes optional ("Added weight") — leave it blank for plain bodyweight, or fill it in for a weighted variation. These show as BW×12 or BW+10kg×12 instead of an odd "0kg".

Session notes

Under Notes, tick the checkbox next to a line once you're done typing it — a new blank line appears below for the next note. This is per-session: it's attached to that one specific logged workout only.

Overall (global) exercise notes

Open any exercise's history and you'll see a Note box above the graphs. Unlike session notes, this one is a single note that always stays attached to that exercise — good for form cues, injury reminders, or equipment settings you want visible every time. Click the text to edit it, click away (or press Enter) to save.

Graphs and how to read them

Top Set Weight plots your heaviest set each session over time — the clearest view of raw strength progress. Average Volume plots weight × reps averaged across a session's sets — useful for spotting whether you're doing more total work even when the top weight hasn't moved. Drag the small handles on a chart's edges to resize it.

In the exercise list, each card shows a ▲ green badge when your estimated 1-rep max has gone up since your first logged session, or a ▼ red badge when it's down. A PR tag on a session means it was your best-ever top set for that exercise up to that point.

Dashboard

The Dashboard tab (your default landing tab) has four views:

  • Session — full breakdown of one day: every exercise, sets, top set, volume, notes, and any PRs. Use Prev/Next to step through days you actually trained.
  • Weekly — totals, volume by split, top exercises, PRs, and a day-by-day list for the selected week, plus the % change vs. the previous week.
  • Monthly — everything Weekly has, plus a consistency % (sessions vs. trainable days), a training calendar heatmap, most-improved exercise, new exercises tried, and an optional transformation-photo strip for that month (toggle it on — it's hidden by default).
  • Custom — pick any two dates and get the same detailed breakdown, compared against an equal-length prior period.

The Week starts Mon/Sun selector at the top of the Dashboard reshapes the Weekly range and the Monthly calendar. Every number on the Dashboard follows your KG/LB unit setting automatically.

Rest Days

Pick the day(s) you plan to rest each week. These days won't break your streak even without a logged workout.
