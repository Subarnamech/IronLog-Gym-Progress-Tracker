HOW TO USE IRON LOG

Account & sync

You need to be logged in to use Iron Log — Log In or Sign Up on the first screen. Everything saves locally on your device first, so it works with no internet, then syncs to your account whenever you're online. Watch the sync badge in the header (next to Log out) — it shows Synced, Syncing…, or Offline — saved locally. Log into the same account on another device to see the same data there.

Logging a set

Pick a Split (e.g. Push Day) and Exercise, fill in weight and reps for as many of the three set slots as you did, set the date, and hit LOG SET. Iron Log groups sets by exercise and date automatically.

Tracking progress

Click any exercise on the right to see its history, estimated 1-rep max trend, and average volume over time. Use the split tabs above the exercise list to filter by training day. Drag the small handles on the edges of each chart to resize its width or height.

Selecting & deleting

Click Select above the exercise list to check off exercises and delete several at once. Inside an exercise's history table, use the row checkboxes the same way to bulk-delete individual sessions.

Streak

The streak card counts consecutive days you either logged a workout or marked as a rest day. Click ⚙ Rest Days to tell Iron Log which day(s) of the week you rest, so those days don't break your streak.

Units

Toggle Units in the header to switch the whole app between kg and lb — your logged numbers convert automatically. Numbers are always stored internally in kg, so switching units never changes your actual data.

Undo / Redo

Undo and Redo in the header (or Ctrl/Cmd+Z and Ctrl/Cmd+Y) step back and forward through your last several edits — handy if you log or delete something by mistake.

Backups & transfers

Use Export JSON for a full backup you can re-import later with Import JSON, or Export CSV to open your sets in a spreadsheet — you can also bring a CSV back in with Import CSV as long as it has exercise, date, split, set, weight, and reps columns. JSON/CSV exports always use kg regardless of your unit toggle, so backups stay consistent. Reset All wipes your logged data — use with care.

Transformation log

Switch to the Transformation Log tab to upload progress photos. Set an interval (in days) and Iron Log will pull out one photo closest to each interval mark so you can see change over time, alongside a full gallery of everything you've uploaded.
